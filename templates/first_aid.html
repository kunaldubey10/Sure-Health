First Aid Guide

Essential first aid procedures for common emergencies

Emergency Numbers

Ambulance: 102 | Police: 100 | Fire: 101 | Women Helpline: 1091 | Child Helpline: 1098

When to use: Person is unconscious and not breathing
  1. Call emergency services immediately (102)
  2. Place person on firm, flat surface
  3. Position your hands in the center of chest
  4. Push hard and fast - at least 2 inches deep
  5. Give 30 chest compressions at 100-120 per minute
  6. Give 2 rescue breaths
  7. Continue 30 compressions and 2 breaths until help arrives
Remember: Push hard, push fast, allow full chest recoil

When to use: Person cannot cough, speak, or breathe
  1. Stand behind the person
  2. Make a fist with one hand, place it above navel
  3. Grasp fist with other hand
  4. Give quick, upward thrusts into abdomen
  5. Repeat until object is dislodged
  6. Call 102 if object doesn't come out
For infants: Use back blows and chest thrusts, not abdominal thrusts

When to use: Heavy bleeding that won't stop
  1. Call 102 immediately for severe bleeding
  2. Have person lie down and stay calm
  3. Remove any visible dirt/debris (don't remove embedded objects)
  4. Apply firm, direct pressure with clean cloth
  5. Maintain pressure for 15-20 minutes without checking
  6. If blood soaks through, add more cloth on top
  7. Elevate injured area above heart if possible
  8. Apply bandage firmly once bleeding stops

For minor burns:
  1. Cool burn with cool (not cold) running water for 10-20 minutes
  2. Remove jewelry and tight clothing before swelling
  3. Apply aloe vera or burn cream
  4. Cover with sterile, non-stick bandage
  5. Take over-the-counter pain reliever if needed
Call 102 for: Burns larger than 3 inches, deep burns, burns on face/hands/feet/joints, electrical/chemical burns
DON'T: Use ice, butter, or ointments on serious burns. Don't break blisters.

Signs: Severe pain, swelling, deformity, inability to move
  1. Call 102 for serious fractures
  2. Don't move the person unless necessary
  3. Immobilize the injured area
  4. Apply ice packs to reduce swelling (wrapped in cloth)
  5. Treat for shock - keep person warm and lying down
  6. If bone breaks through skin, don't push it back

Signs: Chest pain, shortness of breath, nausea, cold sweat
  1. Call 102 IMMEDIATELY
  2. Have person sit down and rest
  3. Loosen any tight clothing
  4. If person takes heart medication, help them take it
  5. Give aspirin if person is conscious and not allergic (chew, don't swallow)
  6. If unconscious and not breathing, begin CPR
  7. Stay with person until help arrives

Use FAST to identify stroke:
  • F - Face: Ask person to smile. Does one side droop?
  • A - Arms: Ask to raise both arms. Does one drift down?
  • S - Speech: Ask to repeat a phrase. Is speech slurred?
  • T - Time: If any of above, call 102 immediately!

  2. Note time when symptoms started
  3. Keep person comfortable and calm
  4. Don't give food, drink, or medication

  2. Try to identify the poison
  3. If unconscious, position on side
  4. Don't induce vomiting unless told by medical professional
  5. If poison on skin, remove contaminated clothing and rinse with water
  6. If inhaled poison, get to fresh air immediately

  7. Call 102 if seizure lasts more than 5 minutes

Essential First Aid Kit Items

  • Adhesive bandages (various sizes)
  • Sterile gauze pads
  • Medical tape
  • Antiseptic wipes
  • Antibiotic ointment
  • Scissors
  • Tweezers
  • Disposable gloves
  • Thermometer
  • Pain relievers (aspirin, ibuprofen)
  • Elastic bandage
  • Instant cold pack
  • CPR face shield
  • Emergency contact numbers
