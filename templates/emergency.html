When to Call 102

Call emergency ambulance service immediately if you or someone else is experiencing:

  • Chest pain or pressure lasting more than a few minutes
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Severe bleeding that won't stop
  • Loss of consciousness or fainting
  • Sudden severe headache or confusion
  • Poisoning or drug overdose
  • Severe allergic reaction
  • Stroke symptoms (face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty)

Quick First Aid Guide

Heart Attack

Symptoms: Chest pain, shortness of breath, nausea, cold sweat

Action:

  1. Call 102 immediately
  2. Have person sit down and rest
  3. Loosen tight clothing
  4. Give aspirin if not allergic (chew one tablet)
  5. Begin CPR if person becomes unconscious
Stroke (F.A.S.T.)

Symptoms:

  • Face: Drooping on one side
  • Arms: Weakness in one arm
  • Speech: Slurred or strange speech
  • Time: Call 102 immediately

Action: Call 102. Note the time symptoms started. Do not give food or drink.

Severe Bleeding

Action:

  1. Call 102 for severe bleeding
  2. Apply direct pressure with clean cloth
  3. Maintain pressure for 10-15 minutes
  4. Elevate the injured area above heart if possible
  5. Don't remove cloth if blood soaks through, add more layers
Burns

Action:

  1. Remove from heat source immediately
  2. Cool burn with running water for 10-20 minutes
  3. Cover with sterile, non-stick bandage
  4. Do NOT apply ice, butter, or ointments
  5. Seek medical help for severe burns
Allergic Reaction

Severe symptoms: Difficulty breathing, swelling of throat, rapid pulse

Action:

  1. Call 102 immediately for severe reactions
  2. Use EpiPen if available (inject into outer thigh)
  3. Have person lie down and elevate legs
  4. Loosen tight clothing
  5. Don't give anything to drink if breathing difficulty
Choking

Action (Heimlich Maneuver):

  1. Stand behind the person
  2. Make a fist below ribcage, above navel
  3. Grasp fist with other hand
  4. Give quick upward thrusts
  5. Repeat until object is dislodged
  6. Call 102 if unsuccessful

Emergency Preparedness Checklist

Essential First Aid Kit Items:
  • Adhesive bandages (various sizes)
  • Sterile gauze pads
  • Adhesive tape
  • Antiseptic wipes
  • Antibiotic ointment
  • Pain relievers (aspirin, ibuprofen)
  • Scissors and tweezers
  • Thermometer
  • Disposable gloves
Important Information to Have Ready:
  • List of current medications
  • Allergies and medical conditions
  • Emergency contact numbers
  • Primary care physician contact
  • Health insurance information
  • Blood type
  • Recent medical history
